Patient Information

Sedation Instructions

Before and after your sedation appointment — please read carefully
Section One
Before Your Appointment
1
Please arrive 15 minutes before your appointment.
2
Take your regular medication at the usual times with a small amount of water.
3
Asthma sufferers: please bring your inhalers. Insulin-dependent diabetics: please bring your blood glucose monitoring device.
4
Avoid smoking and alcohol for 24 hours before your appointment. Recreational drugs are not permitted for 48 hours before treatment.
5
Please empty your bladder before going into the surgery.
6
Please wear loose fitting clothing to allow access to the forearm and elbow for cannulation and monitoring. Avoid heavy make-up, nail varnish and jewellery. Wear flat shoes as you may be slightly unsteady afterwards.
7
If you wear contact lenses, please bring your lens container — you may be asked to remove them.
8
Please arrange for a responsible, able-bodied adult to escort you home, preferably by car or taxi, and to stay with you for a few hours. Guidelines do not allow us to send you home on your own.
9
If you have children at home, you will need to arrange for your escort or another carer to supervise them for the rest of the day.
10
Do not eat any food for 6 hours before the appointment. You may only drink water up to 2 hours before. If you are unable to comply with these instructions, please contact Complete Dentistry.
Section Two
After Your Appointment
1
The sedation may produce temporary amnesia, which rarely lasts more than a few hours.
2
A responsible able-bodied adult will be required to escort you home after the procedure, either by car or taxi. This person will also receive instructions about your aftercare and should ideally remain with you for the remainder of the day.
3
Take your regular medication as usual.
4
Please do not take alcohol for the remainder of the day.
5
Avoid breastfeeding for 24 hours afterwards.
6
It can take up to 24 hours for the sedative drugs to be eliminated from your body and for you to fully recover. Therefore, however well you may feel, please do not:
For the remainder of the day, do not:
  • Drive a vehicle (insurance will be void) or cycle
  • Use electrical equipment, cook, or operate machinery
  • Care for children or dependent adults
  • Make important decisions, sign important documents or perform complicated tasks
  • Use alcohol, sleeping tablets, tobacco or recreational drugs
⚠
We do not anticipate you having any complications but should you become concerned about anything, however trivial, please contact us. Practice: 01883 622222
