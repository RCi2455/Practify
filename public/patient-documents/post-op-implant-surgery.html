Post Operative Instructions

Following Implant Surgery
Following your implant surgery, please follow these instructions carefully to promote healing and protect your implant.
1
As soon as possible after surgery, an ice pack placed over the area will help reduce swelling. Use the pack provided, wrapped in a tea towel. This is only useful on the day of surgery.
2
If bleeding reoccurs, bite on a gauze pack placed directly over the wound. Keep your teeth together with firm pressure for at least 20 minutes. Sit upright.
3
No mouthwash for 2 days, then rinse after meals. Dissolve a teaspoon of salt in a tumbler of warm water. Over 5 minutes, repeatedly hold a mouthful over the operation site without swishing.
4
Swelling is to be expected in most cases and will reach a maximum at 48 hours after surgery.
5
Avoid sucking at or interfering with the wound.
6
Avoid smoking for 48 hours.
7
Avoid strenuous activity for 3–4 days.
8
Avoid alcohol for 48 hours.
9
Avoid hot fluids. Choose cool drinks and soft or minced foods for 24 hours after the procedure.
10
Pain killing tablets (Paracetamol, Ibuprofen) to be taken as directed on the packet.
11
If you wear dentures, leave them out as much as possible.
12
Do not chew on the area where the implants have been placed for the first 6 weeks after surgery.
13
Follow a soft diet (soup, scrambled egg, pasta) for the next few days. Avoid spicy foods.
⚠
If you need further advice or experience any complications, please contact us promptly. Practice: 01883 622222
