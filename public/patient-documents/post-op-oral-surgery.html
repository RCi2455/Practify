Post Operative Instructions

Following Oral Surgery or Tooth Extraction
A clean and healthy mouth aids healing.
Rest
Rest for a few hours following treatment and avoid strenuous exercise.
Rinsing
Do not rinse for at least 24 hours after the last time the area bled. For several days following treatment, rinse your mouth gently after meals and before retiring using a mouthwash made by dissolving ½ teaspoon of salt in a tumbler of water as hot as you can tolerate. Hold the solution in your mouth over the operation site without swishing for several minutes, discard and repeat 2–3 times.
Brushing
Brush the rest of your teeth as normal but avoid the tooth either side of the socket for the first week.
Food & Drink
Avoid hot fluids, alcohol, smoking, hard or chewy foods for 24 hours. Choose cool drinks and soft or minced foods. Avoid sucking at or interfering with the wound.
Bleeding
If bleeding should recur, bite on a gauze pack or rolled-up damp handkerchief directly over the wound. Keep your teeth together with firm pressure for at least 10 minutes. Sit upright.
Pain Relief
Swelling is to be expected in most cases. Pain killing tablets to be taken as directed.
Ibuprofen
Paracetamol
⚠
If excessive bleeding, undue pain or other symptoms occur, contact your Dental Surgeon for advice without delay. Practice: 01883 622222
